Mastering Multiple Choice

Method 1 — read deeply, read smartly

  1. Read the questions and not the options before you read the text or listen to the audio.
  2. Find your own answer in the text or the audio.
  3. Then find the option that matches your answer most closely — the words won't be the same but the meaning will be.

Method 2 — backwards method

If Step 1 fails or takes more than a couple of minutes, try the backwards method.

  • Most likely, one answer will have information that is not in the test — eliminate it.
  • Most likely, one answer will have a lot of words or phrases similar to the text — too similar, so be suspicious. It may be saying the opposite thing or talking about a different point. Eliminate it.
  • You probably have two answers that are true to the text and answer the question. Which one answers the question better? That’s your answer.

Mastering Matching Paragrahs

Mastering Multiple Choice Questions.

Matching Paragraphs — step-by-step method

  • If you have fewer paragraphs than questions, start with the paragraphs.
  • If you have fewer questions than paragraphs, start with the questions.

Assume we have fewer paragraphs than questions (the most common case):

  1. Read the first paragraph only, then read all the questions and mark all the possibilities.
  2. If it’s a perfect match, mark it as the answer and cross it out.
  3. If you’re not sure, put a question mark and move on.
  4. Read the second paragraph, then read all the questions except the ones you have crossed out.
  5. Continue until all the paragraphs are finished.
  6. If you don’t find any questions that match a paragraph, keep going. By the end of the reading, you should have most of the answers.

Method 2 — backwards method

If Step 1 fails or takes more than a couple of minutes, try the backwards method.

  • Most likely, one answer will have information that is not in the test — eliminate it.
  • Most likely, one answer will have a lot of words or phrases similar to the text — too similar, so be suspicious. It may be saying the opposite thing or talking about a different point. Eliminate it.
  • You probably have two answers that are true to the text and answer the question. Which one answers the question better? That’s your answer.
